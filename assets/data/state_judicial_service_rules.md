**Last updated: 17 February 2026** | Compiled for **Nyaya-Uday App**

## Important Notes

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Supreme Court Mandate: 3-Year Practice Requirement

> **All India Judges Association & Ors. v. Union of India & Ors.** *(Decided 20 May 2025)*
>
> The Supreme Court **restored** the mandatory **3 years of legal practice** for entry-level judicial posts (Civil Judge / Junior Division).
>
> **Key points:**
> 1. **Applies prospectively** ‚Äî only to recruitments notified after 20 May 2025
> 2. **Certification** ‚Äî endorsed by Principal Judicial Officer or senior advocate (10+ years) countersigned by PJO
> 3. **Practice period** ‚Äî calculated from date of provisional Bar Council enrollment
> 4. **Law clerk experience counts** ‚Äî time as law clerk to a judge/judicial officer is included
> 5. **Nov 2025 clarification** ‚Äî serving judicial officers (appointed before 20-05-2025) are exempt when applying to another state's judicial service (if 3+ years judicial service in current cadre)
> 6. **Review petition** ‚Äî filed June 2025 requesting implementation from 2027 ‚Äî status pending
>
> **This ruling overrides older state rules that did not require practice.** All High Courts and state governments are directed to amend their service rules accordingly.

### üìå Always Verify Before Applying

1. Check the **latest recruitment notification** from your state's High Court or PSC
2. Age relaxations: SC/ST typically +5 years, OBC +3 years, PwD up to +10 years
3. Maximum attempts may apply (e.g., UP: 4 for General)
4. Some states conduct exams via PSC (Kerala, Karnataka, Odisha, HP, WB), others via HC directly
5. Links marked ‚ö†Ô∏è may still work in a browser ‚Äî try directly if automated check failed
